Annotation Rules (English Version)

Objective

Label clips from animation and categorize them as usable or unusable by segments. Usable clips must include at least one usable segment. Usable segments must meet all the following rules.

Detailed Annotation Rules

1. Front Face Requirements

  • Rotation Angle:
    • Horizontal (left/right) deviation should not exceed 30 degrees.
    • Vertical (up/down) deviation should not exceed 20 degrees.
  • Visible Features: The eyes, nose, and mouth must be fully visible and unobstructed.
  • Face Proportion:
    • The face must occupy at least 10% of each frame in the segment.
    • Natural Expression: Facial expressions should be within a normal range.

2. Voice-to-Face Match

  • The voice must correspond to the visible face in the frame, ensuring it is the same character speaking.
  • The voice should be clear and last for at least 3 seconds.
  • No overlapping voices from other characters or vocal background noise should be present.

3. Background and Interference

  • Background Requirements:
    • The background should be simple and not overly distracting, such as battle scenes, fast-moving objects, or intense lighting changes.
  • Visual Interference:
    • No other characters (e.g., side profiles, back views) or large portions of non-target character bodies should appear in the frame.
    • Faces should not be obscured by objects (e.g., masks, goggles, bandages, or excessive scars).
    • Movements should not be too large, as rapid motion may cause facial blurring.

4. Duration Requirement

  • Minimum duration is 3 seconds, with no upper limit.

5. Annotation Requirements

  • All clips must be labeled, regardless of their usability:
    • Usable: Meets the above rules and is of high quality for the dataset.
    • Unusable: Does not meet the above rules or has significant quality issues.
